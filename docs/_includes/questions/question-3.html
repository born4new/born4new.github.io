#3 - In which towns can we find Good Cops/Bad Cops in the USA?

We then want to understand in which cities our good cops/bad cops are located in the US.

On this boxplot we can see Plano is not doing good at all but one should remember that this city has very few datapoints. Cities such as Nashville and Charlotte display right-heavy tailed distributions. Interestingly in some cities (Charlotte, Nashville) there seem to be quite a few officers with a racial bias which jumps as much as 2 times away the length of Interquatile range (Q3 - Q1) from the third quartile. This means in those cities there are officers which present a particularly high racial bias in comparison to their peers.

The maps that follow show the racial bias of officers accross the USA in multiple cities. One can play with the quantile selector in order to visualize what is the racial bias in each city for a specific quantile. Interestingly even though there are lot of neighbouring cities near Charlotte (Eastern part of the USA) all those cities have notably different racial biases. One of those cities displays a racial bias at the bottom-most part of the range of existing racial bias, when another city shows a racial bias which is at the top-most part of the racial bias range of values. Sadly we do not have enough cities' data to draw big conclusions about a general tendency of racial bias in certain regions of the USA. But those disparities of the racial bias values in close by cities either suggest that we have outliers or that cities in the USA generally have different law enforcement schemes. Maybe the chain of commands are different in those cities and this is what influences the racial bias of officers the most?
