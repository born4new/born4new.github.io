#1 - What does a typical cop look like?

We first want to understand the distribution of our data, namely, the distribution of the 4'584'165 police officers we have at our disposal in the original dataset.

As visible on the left-hand side, most police officers are in their 20s and are in their first year of work.

The age is pretty well distributed between 25 and 50-years-old otherwise: we have 68% of all police officers in between those two ages.

As for the overall experience, 44% of all officers are in their first year in the line of duty (57% in their first three years, and 64% in their five years)

Now, to the genders. Most police officers are men with an overwhelming majority at 87%.

Women are represented at 6.28%, and the remainder is unknown: data has not been provided in the initial dataset.

The races distribution look disproportionate at first sight, but is actually very close to the distribution of races in the united states.

Indeed, according to US census of 2019, there were 76% of whites, 13.4% of blacks, and 18.5% of hispanics.
